Open-source bundle API for Turkish news. This repository serves to the back-end of the project. For client side, click here.

   

Okuryazar API

An API designed to be used in a potential bundle application, which stores news and columns in Turkish newspapers in an SQL database.

Currently supported newspapers: Cumhuriyet, Takvim, Sözcü, Milliyet

Endpoints:

Newspaper params Category params
sozcu gundem
takvim dunya
milliyet ekonomi
cumhuriyet spor
URL Purpose
http://inf303.herokuapp.com/okuryazar-api/get/news/all Retrieves all news saved in DB from 4 newspapers in the last 3 days.
http://inf303.herokuapp.com/okuryazar-api/get/news/:newspaper Retrieves all news saved in DB from a specific newspaper in the last 3 days.
http://inf303.herokuapp.com/okuryazar-api/get/news/:newspaper/:category Retrieves all news saved in DB from a specific newspaper and category in the last 3 days.
http://inf303.herokuapp.com/okuryazar-api/get/article/:articleId Retrieves the specific article with the given ID.
http://inf303.herokuapp.com/okuryazar-api/search/:query Searches the news and articles for the given query.

Okuryazar Client

The client-side of the Okuryazar project is developed using TypeScript, Vue.js, Tailwind CSS, and PostgreSQL.

Features:

  • Retrieve news and articles
  • Search for specific content
  • Filter news by category

Conclusion

Our semester project, the Okuryazar newspaper app, has been a significant achievement for our team. We successfully developed a comprehensive solution for accessing Turkish news from multiple newspapers in one convenient application. By creating the Okuryazar API and integrating it with our client-side interface, we have built a robust system that delivers the latest news and articles to our users.

Throughout the development process, we prioritized collaboration and effective communication within our team. Regular meetings and open discussions allowed us to address challenges, make informed decisions, and ensure the project's progress. Our collective efforts resulted in a well-designed app architecture that leverages the power of Python, Flask, Vue.js, and Tailwind CSS.

The Okuryazar app offers several key features, such as retrieving news and articles, searching for specific content, and filtering news by category. These features, combined with our user-friendly interface, provide a seamless and engaging experience for our users.

In conclusion, we are proud of the outcome of our semester project—the Okuryazar newspaper app. It not only showcases our technical skills but also demonstrates our ability to collaborate effectively as a team. We hope that Okuryazar will become a valuable resource for individuals seeking to stay informed about Turkish news.
